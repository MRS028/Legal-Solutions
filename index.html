Frequently Asked Questions

Below are answers to some common legal questions. For specific advice regarding your situation, please schedule a free consultation.

1. What is the difference between civil and criminal law?

Civil law deals with disputes between individuals or organizations, typically seeking compensation. Criminal law, on the other hand, involves the government prosecuting an individual for an act classified as a crime, with potential penalties including fines or imprisonment.

2. How much does it cost to hire a lawyer?

Legal fees vary greatly depending on the lawyer's experience, the complexity of the case, and the firm's billing structure (e.g., hourly, flat fee, contingency). We offer a free initial consultation to discuss your case and provide a clear fee structure.

3. What should I bring to my first consultation?

Bring all relevant documents related to your case, such as contracts, police reports, correspondence, and a timeline of events. Also, prepare a list of questions you have for the attorney. This will help make the consultation as productive as possible.

4. Do I always need a lawyer to go to court?

While you can represent yourself (pro se), it is highly advisable to have legal representation, especially in complex cases. An experienced lawyer understands legal procedures, rules of evidence, and can build a strong strategic argument on your behalf, significantly increasing your chances of a favorable outcome.
